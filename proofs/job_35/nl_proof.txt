# Proof Sketch: Nonlinear Stability of the Kerr Black Hole

## Preliminary Critique of the Reviewed Sketch

The reviewed sketch is, in my judgment, mathematically honest and largely correct. Its principal virtues are:

- It correctly distinguishes the *full* conjecture (arbitrary subextremal Kerr, $|a|<m$) from the *theorem actually proved* (slowly rotating Kerr, $|a|/m \ll 1$).
- It correctly identifies the GCM (generally covariant modulated) framework, the dual-gauge strategy, and the Teukolsky-to-Regge–Wheeler reduction as the conceptual core.
- It correctly notes that the Dafermos–Holzegel–Rodnianski–Taylor result concerns Schwarzschild, not Kerr.

However, several improvements are warranted:

1. **The role of the Hawking–Penrose causal structure** and the precise statement of "global closeness" are under-articulated. One should make explicit that the conclusion is about the *exterior* region $J^-(\mathcal I^+)\cap J^+(\Sigma)$, not the maximal Cauchy development (which generically contains a singularity).
2. **The Chandrasekhar/Teukolsky–Starobinsky reduction** in Kerr is more subtle than in Schwarzschild because of the non-integrability of the principal null distribution; this point deserves elaboration via Giorgi–Klainerman–Szeftel's tensorial wave system.
3. **The "second-order Teukolsky"** quantities $\mathfrak{q}$ and $\mathfrak{p}$ (rank-2 horizontal traceless tensors) are essential and were merely alluded to.
4. The reviewed sketch under-states *why* the smallness $|a|/m\ll 1$ is needed: the obstruction is the *trapping* on the Kerr photon shell, where superradiance and trapping interact, and where the Carter operator's positivity in the Morawetz estimate degenerates as $|a|/m\to 1$.
5. The literature update should mention that as of 2024–2026, the *linear* stability for the full subextremal range $|a|<m$ has been essentially established (Shlapentokh-Rothman–Teixeira da Costa; Dafermos–Rodnianski–Shlapentokh-Rothman), while *nonlinear* stability beyond slow rotation remains open.

I now give an improved proof sketch.

---

## Precise Statement (Slowly Rotating Case)

**Theorem (Klainerman–Szeftel, with Giorgi–Klainerman–Szeftel for the linear part).** *Fix $m_0>0$ and $a_0\in\mathbb R$ with $|a_0|/m_0$ sufficiently small. There exist $\varepsilon_0>0$ and an integer $N$ such that for any vacuum asymptotically flat initial data set $(\Sigma,g,k)$ with*
$$\|(g,k) - (g_{Kerr(a_0,m_0)},k_{Kerr(a_0,m_0)})\|_{H^N_{\mathrm{wt}}(\Sigma)} < \varepsilon < \varepsilon_0,$$
*the future maximal globally hyperbolic development $(\mathcal M,\mathbf g)$ has the following properties:*

(i) *$\mathcal M$ contains a complete future null infinity $\mathcal I^+$ and a regular future event horizon $\mathcal H^+$;*

(ii) *In the exterior $\mathcal E := J^-(\mathcal I^+)\cap J^+(\Sigma)$, $\mathbf g$ is globally close to $g_{Kerr(a_0,m_0)}$;*

(iii) *There exist final parameters $(a_\infty,m_\infty)$ with $|a_\infty-a_0|+|m_\infty-m_0|\lesssim\varepsilon$ such that on each outgoing null cone $\mathbf g - g_{Kerr(a_\infty,m_\infty)}$ decays at quantitative polynomial rates as one approaches $\mathcal I^+$ and $\mathcal H^+$.*

The unrestricted *Kerr nonlinear stability conjecture* asserts the same for any $|a_0|<m_0$.

## Proof Sketch

### Step 1. Why one cannot prove convergence to the *original* Kerr.

The Einstein vacuum equations $\mathrm{Ric}(\mathbf g)=0$ admit, around any Kerr background, exact infinitesimal deformations corresponding to: changes of mass $\delta m$, changes of angular momentum vector $\delta\vec J\in\mathbb R^3$, boosts/translations of the center of mass, and infinitesimal diffeomorphisms (pure gauge). These are *non-decaying* solutions of the linearized system. Consequently the only meaningful nonlinear statement is *orbital* stability: $\mathbf g$ converges to *some* nearby Kerr along the moduli space $\{Kerr(a,m): |a|<m, m>0\}$, modulo gauge.

### Step 2. Modulation: the GCM construction.

Tracking the moduli requires defining, at every "instant" along the evolution, a Kerr reference $Kerr(\tilde a(\cdot),\tilde m(\cdot))$ and a gauge identifying our spacetime with a perturbation of it. Klainerman–Szeftel achieve this via *generally covariant modulated* spheres: codimension-two spacelike spheres $S$ on which one imposes:

- The Hawking mass and angular momentum aspect take prescribed values $(\tilde m,\tilde a)$.
- Specific spherical-harmonic ($\ell=0,1$) components of the Ricci coefficients $\mathrm{tr}\chi$, $\mathrm{tr}\underline\chi$, and the mass aspect $\mu$ vanish.
- The frame is normalized to be "as close as possible" to the Kerr principal null frame.

These conditions are *geometric* (frame-independent up to the residual finite-dimensional ambiguity), and an implicit-function argument shows they uniquely determine $(\tilde a,\tilde m)$ and the GCM sphere within a small family.

### Step 3. The teleological GCM hypersurface $\Sigma_*$.

Rather than evolve from $\Sigma$ forward, one constructs a *future* spacelike hypersurface $\Sigma_*$ as follows. Start from a far-future GCM sphere $S_*$. Let $\Sigma_*$ be the spacelike hypersurface foliated by GCM spheres obtained by sliding $S_*$ along an inward-pointing null direction. This teleological choice, inspired by Christodoulou–Klainerman, ensures that the dangerous low modes are "killed at the future end," after which they cannot reappear by causality.

The bootstrap region $\mathcal R(u_*,r_*)$ is bounded in the past by (a portion of) $\Sigma$, on the right by $\Sigma_*$, on the left by a portion of $\mathcal H^+$, and is foliated by an outgoing null function $u$ and an ingoing null function $\underline u$ adapted to the GCM structure.

### Step 4. Renormalization against modulated Kerr.

Choose a null frame $(e_3,e_4,e_A)$ that, on exact $Kerr(\tilde a,\tilde m)$, reduces to (a smooth perturbation of) the principal null frame of Carter. Decompose the Weyl curvature into its scalar–tensorial components $(\alpha,\beta,\rho,{}^*\rho,\underline\beta,\underline\alpha)$ and the Ricci coefficients into $(\chi,\underline\chi,\zeta,\eta,\underline\eta,\omega,\underline\omega)$. Subtract from each its value on the modulated Kerr: $\check\Gamma := \Gamma - \Gamma^{Kerr}$, $\check R := R - R^{Kerr}$. All checked quantities vanish identically on Kerr.

### Step 5. Two gauges.

The principal null distribution of Kerr is *non-integrable* for $a\neq 0$. This is the fundamental obstacle distinguishing Kerr from Schwarzschild. Klainerman–Szeftel handle it by working in two complementary gauges:

- **Principal geodesic (PG) gauge:** the outgoing null vector $e_4$ is geodesic and $\omega=0$. This gauge is well-suited to wave estimates because $r$ is well-behaved as an affine parameter.
- **Principal temporal (PT) gauge:** built from a timelike vectorfield close to the Hawking–Killing field; well-suited to transport equations and to the construction of GCM spheres.

The two gauges are compared by a small diffeomorphism whose smallness is bootstrapped.

### Step 6. The hyperbolic core: Teukolsky and the second-order Chandrasekhar transformation.

The extreme curvature components $\alpha$ and $\underline\alpha$ are *gauge-invariant* (Bianchi-isolated) and satisfy nonlinear Teukolsky-type wave equations. Following the strategy initiated by Chandrasekhar in the linear theory and made tensorial by Dafermos–Holzegel–Rodnianski (Schwarzschild) and Giorgi–Klainerman–Szeftel (Kerr, slow rotation), one introduces second-order tensorial quantities
$$\mathfrak q := r^4\big[\nabla_3\nabla_3\alpha + \text{lower order}\big], \qquad \mathfrak p := r^4\big[\nabla_4\nabla_4\underline\alpha + \text{l.o.t.}\big],$$
which satisfy *generalized Regge–Wheeler equations*
$$\Box_{\mathbf g}\mathfrak q - V(r,\theta)\mathfrak q = \mathcal N(\mathfrak q,\mathfrak p,\check\Gamma,\check R)$$
with potentials $V$ that are *non-negative* on the trapping set in the slowly rotating regime. This positivity is what allows a Morawetz multiplier argument to close.

### Step 7. Vectorfield estimates.

Apply the vectorfield method to the Regge–Wheeler equation:

- **Red-shift (Dafermos–Rodnianski):** A vectorfield $Y$ transverse to $\mathcal H^+$ provides a positive bulk near the horizon, exploiting the surface gravity $\kappa>0$.
- **$r^p$-hierarchy (Dafermos–Rodnianski):** For $0\le p\le 2$, multipliers of the form $r^p e_4$ yield a hierarchy of weighted energy estimates that, via a pigeonhole argument, give pointwise polynomial decay $\sim u^{-1}$ along $\mathcal I^+$.
- **Morawetz/integrated local energy decay (ILED):** A vectorfield $X = f(r)e_*$ tuned to vanish at the photon sphere yields, after commutation with the Carter tensorial operator $\mathcal Q$ (which captures the hidden symmetry of Kerr), an estimate
$$\int_{\mathcal R}\frac{1}{r^{1+\delta}}|\nabla\mathfrak q|^2 + \frac{1}{r^{3+\delta}}|\mathfrak q|^2 \lesssim \mathcal E[\mathfrak q](0).$$
The Carter operator's commutator with $\Box_{\mathbf g}$ produces an error $O(a^2)$; this is *the* place where smallness of $|a|/m$ enters in an essential, currently irreducible way.

- **Commutation with Killing fields:** $T = \partial_t$ and $\Phi = \partial_\phi$ commute with Kerr; commutator errors with the *true* metric are nonlinear and bootstrap-controlled.

### Step 8. Recovery of the full geometry.

From boundedness and decay of $\mathfrak q,\mathfrak p$:

(a) Invert the Chandrasekhar transformation by a transport–elliptic system to recover decay for $\check\alpha,\check{\underline\alpha}$.

(b) The Bianchi identities $\nabla_3\beta + \cdots = \nabla\rho + \cdots$ etc. are first-order transport equations that propagate decay from $(\check\alpha,\check{\underline\alpha})$ to $(\check\beta,\check{\underline\beta},\check\rho,\check{}^*\rho)$.

(c) The null structure equations $\nabla_4\chi + \cdots = R$ etc. then give decay for the Ricci coefficients, *modulo* their projections onto the spherical harmonics $\ell=0,1$.

(d) The dangerous $\ell=0,1$ projections are *exactly* the modes pinned down by the GCM conditions on $\Sigma_*$. Hence they are controlled.

### Step 9. The bootstrap closure.

Assume on $\mathcal R(u_*)$ all renormalized quantities satisfy the bootstrap norm $\|\cdot\|_{\mathfrak{B}} \le C_B\varepsilon$. The estimates of Steps 7–8 *improve* this to $\le \tfrac12 C_B\varepsilon$ provided $\varepsilon$ and $|a|/m$ are sufficiently small. By a continuity argument in $u_*$, the GCM region extends beyond its presumed terminal slice. Iterating and passing to the limit $u_*\to\infty$ produces the global exterior solution.

### Step 10. The final state and asymptotic completeness.

The modulated mass and angular momentum $(\tilde m(u_*), \tilde a(u_*))$ are Cauchy in $u_*$ (because $\check R\to 0$ at quantitative rates), with limits $(m_\infty,a_\infty)$. Decay of all checked quantities then gives that $\mathbf g \to g_{Kerr(a_\infty,m_\infty)}$ uniformly on each truncated outgoing null cone as $u\to\infty$. Completeness of $\mathcal I^+$ follows from the affine completeness of $e_4$-geodesics, itself a consequence of the $r^p$-hierarchy. Regularity of $\mathcal H^+$ follows from the red-shift estimate.

## Why the Full Conjecture Remains Open

The bottleneck is **trapping in the rapidly rotating regime.** On Kerr, null geodesics that neither escape to $\mathcal I^+$ nor cross $\mathcal H^+$ form a 3-dimensional submanifold ("trapping set," generalizing Schwarzschild's photon sphere at $r=3m$). For $a=0$, this is a single sphere, and a single Morawetz multiplier vanishing there suffices. For $0<|a|/m\ll 1$, trapping spreads to a thin shell whose location depends on the constants of motion, but the Carter integral provides a *positive* commutator allowing ILED. For $|a|/m\to 1$, the trapping shell thickens and superradiance (energy extraction from the ergoregion) intensifies. The crucial coercivity $V(r,\theta)\ge 0$ that underlies the Regge–Wheeler estimate degenerates.

At the *linear* level, this difficulty has been overcome:
- Dafermos–Rodnianski–Shlapentokh-Rothman (2014, scalar wave equation, full subextremal).
- Shlapentokh-Rothman–Teixeira da Costa (2020–2023, Teukolsky equation, full subextremal).
- Andersson–Bäckdahl–Blue–Ma (2019/2025) reducing the full linearized gravity problem on Kerr to integrated Teukolsky estimates.

But translating these *linear* estimates to the *nonlinear* setting requires uniform-in-$a$ Morawetz bounds compatible with the GCM bootstrap, which as of April 2026 remains unestablished. The technical obstruction is that the second-order Chandrasekhar transformation in the full subextremal range produces potentials that change sign and require a frequency-localized analysis incompatible with current physical-space GCM techniques.

## Final Verdict

## Final Verdict

VERDICT: PARTIALLY_PROVED

The conjecture is established by Klainerman–Szeftel and Giorgi–Klainerman–Szeftel for asymptotically flat vacuum perturbations of slowly rotating Kerr ($|a|/m \ll 1$); the full subextremal range $|a|<m$ remains open at the nonlinear level despite recent decisive progress on the linear problem.
