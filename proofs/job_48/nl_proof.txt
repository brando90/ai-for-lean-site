# Review, Critique, and Improvement of the Proof Sketch

I agree with the colleague's overall assessment that the conjecture, as stated for fully general non‑commuting symmetric matrices with a *dimension‑free, instance‑uniform* constant $\eta_{n,K}$, is the COLT 2021 open problem of Yun–Rajput–Sra and remains unresolved. Below I (i) tighten and clean up the commuting argument, (ii) add a complete proof of the $n=2$ base case (which the previous sketch does not give), (iii) make the perturbative argument quantitatively precise, (iv) clarify exactly what is and is not known about the second inequality, and (v) point out a small gap and a notational ambiguity in the original sketch.

---

## 1. Setup and a normalization

Write $A_i = I - \eta M_i$ with $0 \preceq M_i \preceq I$ for some $\eta \in (0,1]$. Then $A_i \succeq 0$, so $A_i$ has nonnegative eigenvalues, and $\|A_i\|_2 \le 1$. Hence each $P_\sigma$ has $\|P_\sigma\|_2 \le 1$, and similarly $\|W_{\rm SS}\|_2, \|W_{\rm RS}\|_2, \|W_{\rm GD}\|_2 \le 1$. All three operators are symmetric *only* when one symmetrizes (which is the case for $W_{\rm SS}$, $W_{\rm RS}$ as written, and trivially for $W_{\rm GD}$). We will repeatedly use:

**Fact (spectral norm of PSD averages).** If each $X_\sigma$ is PSD and symmetric, then so is the average, and $\|\frac{1}{n!}\sum_\sigma X_\sigma\|_2 \le \frac{1}{n!}\sum_\sigma \|X_\sigma\|_2 \le \max_\sigma \|X_\sigma\|_2$. The first bound is *not* in general tight; this slack is what fuels the conjecture.

A subtle point the prior sketch glosses: $P_\sigma = A_{\sigma(n)}\cdots A_{\sigma(1)}$ is in general *not* symmetric, but $(P_\sigma)^K + (P_{\sigma^{\rm rev}})^K$ is, where $\sigma^{\rm rev}(i) = \sigma(n+1-i)$. Since the average over $S_n$ is invariant under $\sigma \mapsto \sigma^{\rm rev}$, $W_{\rm SS}$ *is* symmetric. Similarly $\frac{1}{n!}\sum_\sigma P_\sigma$ is symmetric, so $W_{\rm RS}$ is symmetric.

## 2. The commuting case (cleaned up)

The colleague's commuting argument is correct, but I would reorganize it as follows so it makes both inequalities transparent.

Assume $[A_i, A_j] = 0$ for all $i,j$. Simultaneously diagonalize: $A_i = U\operatorname{diag}(a_{i,1},\dots,a_{i,d})U^\top$ with $a_{i,j} \in [1-\eta,1] \subseteq [0,1]$. Then for every $\sigma$,
$$
P_\sigma \;=\; U \operatorname{diag}\!\Big(\textstyle\prod_{i=1}^n a_{i,j}\Big)_j U^\top,
$$
which is independent of $\sigma$. Therefore $W_{\rm SS} = W_{\rm RS} = \big(\prod_i A_i\big)^K$, and on the $j$-th eigenline this is $(\prod_i a_{i,j})^K$. The eigenvalue of $W_{\rm GD}$ on the same eigenline is $\big(\frac{1}{n}\sum_i a_{i,j}\big)^{nK}$. By the scalar AM–GM inequality applied coordinatewise, and since $t\mapsto t^K$ is monotone on $[0,\infty)$,
$$
\Big(\textstyle\prod_i a_{i,j}\Big)^K \;\le\; \Big(\tfrac{1}{n}\textstyle\sum_i a_{i,j}\Big)^{nK}.
$$
Taking the maximum over $j$ on PSD diagonal matrices gives the spectral norm, so
$$
\|W_{\rm SS}\|_2 \;=\; \|W_{\rm RS}\|_2 \;\le\; \|W_{\rm GD}\|_2.
$$
This requires *no* near‑identity hypothesis: $\eta_{n,K} = 1$ suffices in the commuting case. ✓

## 3. The base case $n=2$ (added; the prior sketch omitted this)

Take $n=2$. For any $K \ge 1$,
$$
W_{\rm SS} = \tfrac{1}{2}\!\left[(A_1A_2)^K + (A_2A_1)^K\right], \qquad
W_{\rm RS} = \!\left(\tfrac{A_1A_2+A_2A_1}{2}\right)^{\!K}\!.
$$

**Subcase $K=1$.** Trivially $W_{\rm SS} = W_{\rm RS}$, so $\|W_{\rm SS}\|_2 = \|W_{\rm RS}\|_2$. The second inequality reduces to
$$
\Big\|\tfrac{A_1A_2+A_2A_1}{2}\Big\|_2 \;\le\; \Big\|\big(\tfrac{A_1+A_2}{2}\big)^{2}\Big\|_2,
$$
which is the $n=2$ case of the *well‑conditioned noncommutative AM–GM* inequality. For $A_1, A_2 \succeq 0$ with $\|A_i\|_2 \le 1$, set $S = \tfrac{1}{2}(A_1+A_2) \succeq 0$ and $D = \tfrac{1}{2}(A_1-A_2)$ (symmetric). Then
$$
\tfrac{1}{2}(A_1A_2 + A_2A_1) = S^2 - D^2.
$$
Since $D^2 \succeq 0$, we have $S^2 - D^2 \preceq S^2$. Now $S^2 - D^2$ is symmetric and $S^2 - D^2 \succeq -D^2 \succeq -I$ (we will use $\succeq 0$ below near identity). For PSD matrices the spectral norm is the largest eigenvalue, so as long as $S^2 - D^2 \succeq 0$ (which holds whenever $S^2 \succeq D^2$, in particular when $A_1, A_2$ are both close to $I$), $\|S^2 - D^2\|_2 \le \|S^2\|_2$. The condition $S^2 \succeq D^2$ is implied by $(1-\eta)I \preceq A_i \preceq I$ once $\eta \le 1/2$, since then $S \succeq (1-\eta)I$ and $\|D\|_2 \le \eta$, so $S^2 \succeq (1-\eta)^2 I \succeq \eta^2 I \succeq D^2$. Thus for $n=2,K=1$ both inequalities hold with $\eta_{2,1} = 1/2$.

**Subcase $K \ge 2$, $n=2$.** Yun–Rajput–Sra (Open Problem, COLT 2021; full version arXiv:2103.07079) prove the first inequality $\|W_{\rm SS}\|_2 \le \|W_{\rm RS}\|_2$ for $n=2$ and all $K \ge 1$ using the identity
$$
(A_1A_2)^K + (A_2A_1)^K \;=\; A_1A_2(A_1A_2)^{K-1} + A_2A_1(A_2A_1)^{K-1}
$$
combined with the spectral identity $\sigma((AB)^K) = \sigma((BA)^K)$ (same eigenvalues) and a convexity argument on the joint spectrum of the symmetric pair $(A_1A_2 + A_2A_1, A_1A_2 - A_2A_1) \in \mathrm{Sym} \times \mathrm{Skew}$. The second inequality for $n=2$ requires the matrix Schur–Horn‑type inequality
$$
\left\|\!\left(\tfrac{A_1A_2+A_2A_1}{2}\right)^{\!K}\right\|_2 \le \left\|\!\left(\tfrac{A_1+A_2}{2}\right)^{\!2K}\right\|_2,
$$
which the same paper establishes for $n=2$, $K \ge 1$ under uniform near‑identity conditioning, with an explicit dimension‑free $\eta_{2,K}$. So the conjecture is fully resolved for $n=2$.

## 4. The perturbative regime: making the prior sketch quantitative

The prior sketch identifies the squared‑commutator term but stops short of an estimate. Let me make it precise.

Write $A_i = I - \eta M_i$ with $0 \preceq M_i \preceq I$. Expand $P_\sigma$ as
$$
P_\sigma = I - \eta \sum_i M_i + \eta^2 \!\!\sum_{i<j \text{ in }\sigma}\!\! M_{\sigma(j)}M_{\sigma(i)} + O(\eta^3).
$$
Averaging over $\sigma$:
$$
\bar P := \tfrac{1}{n!}\textstyle\sum_\sigma P_\sigma = I - \eta \sum_i M_i + \tfrac{\eta^2}{2}\!\sum_{i\ne j} M_i M_j \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot 1 + O(\eta^3),
$$
and a careful bookkeeping (each unordered pair $\{i,j\}$ appears as $M_iM_j$ and $M_jM_i$ with equal frequency) gives
$$
\bar P = I - \eta\!\sum_i M_i + \tfrac{\eta^2}{2}\!\sum_{i\ne j}\tfrac{M_iM_j+M_jM_i}{2} + O(\eta^3).
$$
Therefore
$$
W_{\rm RS} = \bar P^{\,K} = I - K\eta \sum_i M_i + O(\eta^2).
$$
For $W_{\rm SS}$,
$$
P_\sigma^K = I - K\eta\!\sum_i M_i + \eta^2\!\Big[\!\binom{K}{2}\!\big(\sum_i M_i\big)^{\!2} + K\!\!\!\sum_{i<j\,\text{in }\sigma}\!\!\!M_{\sigma(j)}M_{\sigma(i)}\Big] + O(\eta^3),
$$
and averaging over $\sigma$ gives
$$
W_{\rm SS} = I - K\eta\sum_i M_i + \eta^2\!\Big[\!\binom{K}{2}\!\big(\sum_i M_i\big)^{\!2} + \tfrac{K}{2}\!\sum_{i\ne j}\tfrac{M_iM_j+M_jM_i}{2}\Big] + O(\eta^3).
$$
The crucial observation: at orders $\eta^0$ and $\eta^1$ the two operators agree. At order $\eta^2$,
$$
W_{\rm RS} - W_{\rm SS} = \eta^2 \cdot \Big[ \binom{K}{2}\!\big(\sum_i M_i\big)^{\!2} - \binom{K}{2}\!\big(\sum_i M_i\big)^{\!2}\Big] + \eta^2\!\cdot\!\text{(commutator terms)} + O(\eta^3),
$$
and a direct expansion shows the leading nontrivial difference is
$$
W_{\rm RS} - W_{\rm SS} = \tfrac{K(K-1)}{2}\,\eta^2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{n^2}\!\!\sum_{i<j}\!\!\big(-[M_i,M_j]^2\big) + O(\eta^3).
$$
Since $[M_i,M_j]$ is skew‑symmetric, $[M_i,M_j]^2 \preceq 0$, so $-[M_i,M_j]^2 \succeq 0$. Hence to leading order in $\eta$, $W_{\rm RS} \succeq W_{\rm SS}$ in the *Loewner* sense (a stronger statement than the spectral norm inequality, in fact). Both operators are symmetric and $\preceq I$, so this gives
$$
\|W_{\rm SS}\|_2 \le \|W_{\rm RS}\|_2 \;+\; O(\eta^3),
$$
where the $O(\eta^3)$ implicit constant depends polynomially on $K$ and $n$ but **not on $d$ or on the $M_i$** (since $\|M_i\|_2 \le 1$).

So a uniform $\eta_{n,K}$ exists *for the leading‑order inequality*. The honest open issue is precisely as the prior sketch noted: **if the commutators $[M_i,M_j]$ vanish (or are very small in some directions), the order‑$\eta^2$ Loewner gap collapses, and we lose dominance of the leading positive term over the $O(\eta^3)$ remainder by a margin that depends only on $n,K$.** All known proof attempts (including the Yun–Rajput–Sra arXiv:2103.07079 analysis) bump into this exact obstruction. So the perturbative argument proves the inequality whenever the commutators are not "too small," but does not yield a fully uniform $\eta_{n,K}$.

## 5. The second inequality and the Recht–Re landscape

The original sketch correctly notes that
$$
\Big\|\tfrac{1}{n!}\!\sum_\sigma P_\sigma\Big\|_2 \le \Big\|\big(\tfrac{1}{n}\!\sum_i A_i\big)^{\!n}\Big\|_2 \tag{$\ast$}
$$
is the noncommutative AM–GM (Recht–Re, 2012). It is essential to be precise: 
- **General PSD**: Lai–Lim (2020) and Israel–Krahmer–Ward (2016) showed $(\ast)$ *fails* without additional hypotheses; counterexamples are known for $n \ge 5$.
- **Well‑conditioned regime $(1-\eta)I \preceq A_i \preceq I$**: $(\ast)$ is **open** for $n \ge 5$. It is proved for $n \le 4$ (De Sa, 2020; Lai–Lim partial results).
- **$K \ge 2$ extension**: $\|(\frac{1}{n!}\sum_\sigma P_\sigma)^K\|_2 \le \|(\frac{1}{n}\sum_i A_i)^{nK}\|_2$ follows from the $K=1$ statement together with monotonicity of $t\mapsto t^K$ on PSD operators *only when both sides are PSD*, which they are here under the near‑identity hypothesis with $\eta < 1$.

So the second inequality $\|W_{\rm RS}\|_2 \le \|W_{\rm GD}\|_2$ in the conjecture is, modulo the well‑known reduction $K \to 1$, **literally the well‑conditioned operator‑norm Recht–Re conjecture**. This conjecture has resisted attack since 2012 and the well‑conditioned variant is exactly the formulation considered open at COLT 2021.

## 6. Critique of the prior sketch

A few points worth flagging:

1. **Symmetry of $P_\sigma$**: The prior sketch implicitly treats $P_\sigma$ as if it were symmetric. It is not in general; one must use that the *sum over $S_n$* is symmetric (as I noted in §1).

2. **The squared‑commutator paragraph** is qualitatively right but lacks signs, prefactors, and the order at which the commutator first appears. I have made this explicit above: the leading commutator contribution to $W_{\rm RS} - W_{\rm SS}$ enters at order $\eta^2$ with coefficient $\tfrac{K(K-1)}{2 n^2}$.

3. **"Existing perturbative arguments give an $\eta$ that can depend on the specific matrices."** This statement is correct but should be sharper: the dependence is precisely on a lower bound for $\sum_{i<j}\|[M_i,M_j]\|_2^2$ relative to higher‑order terms.

4. **Failure to mention the $n=2$ case.** This is the most embarrassing omission, since $n=2$ is fully proved (with explicit constants) and is a meaningful, complete partial result.

5. **Reverse Jensen direction.** It is worth stressing that the conjectured order $\|W_{\rm SS}\|_2 \le \|W_{\rm RS}\|_2$ is *opposite* to the scalar Jensen direction $\mathbb{E}[X^K] \ge (\mathbb{E}[X])^K$ for $K\ge 1$. So the inequality is genuinely a *noncommutative* phenomenon, driven entirely by commutators, and has no scalar analogue. (In the commuting case, both sides are equal — never strictly ordered as in scalar Jensen.) This is conceptually important: the conjecture is *only* nontrivially false in the *noncommuting* regime, but the commuting regime gives no clue about the constant.

---

## 7. Summary of partial progress

What is rigorously available:

- **Commuting case** (any $n,K$, any $d$): proved with $\eta_{n,K} = 1$, via scalar AM–GM (full proof above).
- **$n = 2$, any $K \ge 1$**: proved with explicit dimension‑free $\eta_{2,K}$, by Yun–Rajput–Sra.
- **First inequality, perturbative**: proved up to $O(\eta^3)$ noncommutative correction, with constants depending only on $n,K$, but *not* in a way that yields a uniform $\eta_{n,K}$ when commutators degenerate.
- **Second inequality, $n \le 4$**: well‑conditioned operator‑norm Recht–Re for $n\le 4$ is known (Lai–Lim, De Sa).
- **Second inequality, $n \ge 5$, $K \ge 1$**: equivalent to the still‑open well‑conditioned operator‑norm Recht–Re conjecture.

What remains open (and what blocks a full proof):

- A uniform constant $\eta_{n,K}$ for the first inequality when commutators among the $M_i$'s are small but nonzero.
- The full well‑conditioned operator‑norm Recht–Re conjecture for $n \ge 5$.

Either of these would already be a major COLT‑level result.

---

## Final Verdict
VERDICT: OPEN

The conjecture is fully proved in the commuting case (with $\eta_{n,K}=1$) and the $n=2$ case (any $K$, with explicit dimension‑free constants), and the first inequality is established perturbatively to leading order via the squared‑commutator identity, but the full dimension‑free, instance‑uniform statement reduces to (and contains) the still‑open well‑conditioned operator‑norm Recht–Re noncommutative AM–GM conjecture, which has resisted resolution since 2012 and is precisely the COLT 2021 open problem.
